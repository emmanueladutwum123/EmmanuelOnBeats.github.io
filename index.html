About Me

Rhythms and Strings: A Versatile Music & Arts Portfolio

Emmanuel Adutwum is a Ghanaian-born, Orange County-based jazz drummer, pianist, guitarist, educator, and conductor whose music uniquely blends jazz, classical, reggae, afrobeats, afrofusion, highlife, pop and the rich traditions of his Ghanaian heritage. His musical journey is a rhythm that began in his childhood, guided by the his lovely mother who early in his childhood played as brass band brigade drummer at the Presbyterian Church of Ghana, Abeka Congregation(now Royal Priesthood Congregation). It was there, within the vibrant walls of the church, that his mother introduced him to a master drummer who laid his foundation. This early training in Ghana's rich gospel tradition became the bedrock of everything in his life.

He honed his craft as a gospel drummer, bringing energy to congregations across Ghana, from Presbyterian and Pentecost to ICGS and Family Life International Chapel. This path continued in the United States, where he shared his rhythmic keys and grooves with Pentecost church from Cleveland and Pittsburgh to California, connecting communities through a universal pulse.

Over the past 12 years, his dedication to the drum set has only deepened, and his musical artistry has expanded. He has explored the melodic realms of the guitar and piano, developing a versatile skill set that enables him to navigate a vast sonic landscape. His repertoire is a reflection of his journey, seamlessly weaving together Jazz, Gospel, Hillsong, Highlife, Afrobeat, Afrofusion, Reggae, Pop, and R&B.

Through his music, Emmanuel Adutwum strives to foster connection and spread messages of hope, love, and unity. His innovative approach to blending musical traditions continues to inspire audiences, creating a powerful, cross-cultural dialogue through his artistry.

Special Events

Music In Common Performance

I was honored to receive a wonderful invitation from musicincommon.org to perform with my band JazzIsMine at Soka University of America. This special event featured a full band performance followed by my individual solo drums performance, showcasing the power of music to bring people together across cultural divides.

Meeting Grammy Jazz Artists

Through the gracious arrangement by Professor Michael Golden, I had the incredible opportunity to meet and converse with two distinguished jazz artists: Roberta Gambarini, a two-time Grammy award nominee, and Tamir Hendelman, the legendary jazz pianist. We had an enriching discussion about jazz music, their experiences in the industry, and the evolution of jazz as an art form.

Bridges 2 Business Competition

Special performance at the B2B Competition with my band, featuring unique arrangements of "Isn't She Lovely" and original rhythmic compositions. This event highlighted the intersection of business and creative arts.
